MJ-DA works towards the empowerment of

  • children
  • women
  • families, and
  • other undeserved community groups through integrated development programs.
This association focuses on capacity building and encourages genuine participation
of beneficiaries to the effect that target communities become HIV/AIDS competent and have their livelihood improve.

  • Self reliance
  • Trust worthiness
  • Non-discriminatory
  • Transparency and accountability at all levels
  • Integrity and mutual respect between among stake holders

  • Children centered program
  • Capacity building and networking
  • Holistic /integrated
  • Basic service provision and advocacy
  • Gender mainstreaming
  • Community based
  • Subsidiary and goods governance team work and competence

  • MJ -DA intervenes in two regions Addis Ababa and Southern Nations and Nationalities based at four coordination office
    (Kolfei, Yeka , Hawassa, Dilla) that constitutes nine satellite offices located in Southern nations and nationalities, Sidama zone,
    Gedoi Zone and Halabakulito special woreda. Mary Joy covers 63 Kebeles and has been benefiting over 1,580,475 people directly and indirectly.

The highest governing body of the organisation is the General Assembly which has over 43 members (of which 9 are females and the rest are males). The General Assembly is followed by the Board of Directors which has got 5 members (2 females and 3 males) and the secretariat. The Board of Directors is followed by a senior and extended Management Committee. This comprises of the Executive Director, Program, Finance and Administration Directors, Medical Directors and Area Program Managers who are responsible for undertaking the day to day activities of the organization respectively. A system of monitoring tools is in place and the Board formally communicates with the General Assembly once a year, whilst the Executive Director and the Management reports quarterly to the Board. MJDA consists of experienced professionals from different disciplines in particular from social science, medical, managerial skills and has collective work experience in HIV&AIDS prevention, poverty reduction, and health and education.

  • Work towards reducing the level of poverty through prompting basic social service and improving livelihood of target communities.
  • Prevent the HIV transmission and mitigate the impact of Aids on the target communities with special focus on.
  • infected and affected members of the community both through service delivery and advocacy work.
  • Build institutional capacity of MJDA and target communities for effective implementation of the mission of the organization.
